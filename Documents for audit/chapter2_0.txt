Page 2 - The Missing Archives
Mara arrived at the archive gates just as the bells struck nine. The doors, normally open, were sealed by the Council’s sigil—a wax impression that shimmered faintly with protective wards. She pressed her palm against the sigil, and the wax melted away, revealing the dim corridors within. The smell of ink and parchment greeted her like an old friend. Her assistant, Leron, appeared from behind a stack of crates, his face pale. “You heard?” he asked. She nodded. Together they stepped into the Archive Hall. The rows that once housed centuries of chronicles were stripped bare. Dust motes drifted in the light like lost words. Mara knelt, brushing her fingers across the stone floor. There—faint but clear—were drag marks leading toward the lower vaults. “Someone moved them,” she whispered. “But why hide history?” Leron’s gaze darted toward the sealed stairway. “Maybe they didn’t hide it,” he said softly. “Maybe they erased it.”
